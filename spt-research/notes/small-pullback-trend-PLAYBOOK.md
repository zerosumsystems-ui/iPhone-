## The setup in one sentence

SPT is the strongest type of trend day — opens at one extreme, closes at the other, with every pullback staying shallow (≤ 20–30% of recent ADR). The scanner fires loudest on the with-trend continuation setups (H1/H2/L1/L2) during these days. Trading it is a pure selectivity problem: the raw population has positive edge, and 9 stacked filters double the per-trade expectancy.

## What DOESN'T work (rejected in research)

- ❌ **2R target** — baseline SPT with-trend at 2R is −0.12R/trade. 90% of winners run past 3R MFE. [[small-pullback-trend-targets-and-urgency-2026-04-18]] §2
- ❌ **Widening stops to 2R** — strictly worse (−0.55R/trade). The extra saved trades are swamped by the wider losses. [[small-pullback-trend-stop-and-timing-2026-04-18]] §1
- ❌ **Breakeven-stop trap** — Brooks' original warning, already priced in by keeping stop at 1R rather than narrowing prematurely.
- ❌ **Time-stop (bar-N)** — bar-20 costs −12R; bar-10 catastrophic (−52R). 96% of winners run past 3R MFE. [[small-pullback-trend-time-stop-2026-04-18]]
- ❌ **Event-triggered exits** (first adverse close, bear trend bar closing below 10EMA, etc.) — all 7 tested variants cost R. Rules fire on genuine SPT texture and shoot 60–65% winners. [[small-pullback-trend-adverse-event-2026-04-18]]
- ❌ **Hard cap at N trades/day** — discards winners on good days. [[small-pullback-trend-short-side-and-daily-gate-2026-04-18]] §2.e
- ❌ **Urgency-rescue of 1st detection** — counter-intuitively, high-urgency 1st detections are the *worst* cell (+0.19R at u≥8). Skip-1st beats every urgency-based rescue. [[small-pullback-trend-ordinal-and-daygate-2026-04-18]] §1
- ❌ **Signal-bar body% filter** — scanner's window-level trend-bar density already captures this. [[small-pullback-trend-robustness-2026-04-18]] §3
- ❌ **Signal-bar ATR band** — U-shape (quiet and explosive both work) but too noisy to harden. [[small-pullback-trend-entry-side-filters-2026-04-18]]
- ❌ **Signal-bar relvol_20 lower bound** (`relvol≥0.7`/`≥0.85`) — actively destroys expectancy. relvol distribution is non-monotonic with a sharp Q2 trap (relvol 0.61–0.85, WR 19%, perR −0.15, DD −9R). Lower-bound filters chop out Q1 (the best cell) without removing Q2. Use as a sizing tag (see "Sizing tags" below), not a binary filter. [[small-pullback-trend-signal-volume-2026-04-19]]

## Key Brooks-grounded intuitions

- **Weak-looking signal bars are diagnostic.** The emotional texture of an SPT is "ugly with-trend bars, compelling bear bars that fail." Your instinct to wait for a clean H2 signal bar before entering is the trap the tape rewards; the scanner sees the pattern you can't.
- **Urgency measures bar texture, not regime maturity.** A loud 1st bar is the climax that fails, not the start of the trend. The 2nd+ detection has already survived one shallow-pullback cycle — that's the actual SPT diagnostic. [[small-pullback-trend-ordinal-and-daygate-2026-04-18]] §8
- **Counter-trend squeeze lands around 11:00 ET.** Bears think the morning trend is exhausted, step in, get squeezed by 12:00–13:00 ET continuation. 9/9 short losses in the 11:00–11:30 ET window. [[small-pullback-trend-short-side-and-daily-gate-2026-04-18]] §1
- **Lunch (11:30–13:30 ET) is the highest-edge window** for SPT entries — do not fade SPT through lunch. [[small-pullback-trend-entry-side-filters-2026-04-18]]
- **Hold to target.** Brooks' warning about "not moving stops to breakeven too early" generalizes to "don't design any mid-trade exit." The upstream filters already remove the trades that deserve early exit.

## The rhythm of SPT

Brooks text: SPT days form 1–2× per month. Pattern Lab data: 8 of 39 trading days deliver 81% of R. That's the expected rhythm — not a daily setup, a rare one. Sizing assumption: 1R risk per trade, ~30 trades / 3.5 months at the full stack, ~+66R expected total, ~+19R / month.

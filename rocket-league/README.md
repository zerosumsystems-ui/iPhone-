# Rocket League — 1000 MMR 3s progression

A phone-readable guide for climbing out of ~1000 MMR in 3s, with a specific focus
on the three mechanics you asked about: **air-roll-through-rings**,
**flip resets**, and **ceiling air dribbles**.

Start here if you keep forgetting YouTube tutorials or losing track of map/pack
codes.

---

## TL;DR

1. **Install BakkesMod** → **Workshop Map Loader plugin** → put Lethamyr's maps
   in the right folder. This is the thing that unblocks "bigger rings".
2. **Lethamyr's ring maps don't have codes** — you download `.udk` files from
   his site and load them through BakkesMod. Training-pack codes (the
   `XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX` ones) are a different system.
3. Follow the 4-week progression below. Don't skip mechanics — flip resets
   without a clean ceiling read just fall to the floor.
4. 70% ranked / 30% training is the ratio that actually moves MMR. Pure
   freeplay stalls out around where you are now.

---

## 1. Get the maps working (the part you're stuck on)

### BakkesMod setup

1. Install **BakkesMod** from `bakkesmod.com` (free; Epic + Steam both work).
2. Launch Rocket League once with BakkesMod running — the `F2` menu should
   appear in the main menu.
3. In the BakkesMod `F2` menu → **Plugins** → **Plugin Manager** → install
   **Workshop & Custom Maps Loader**.

### Lethamyr's maps (including the big rings)

Lethamyr hosts his maps on his own site, not the Steam Workshop, so there is
**no training-pack code** for them.

1. Go to `lethamyr.com/mymaps`.
2. The map you're probably looking for is **"Ring Map: Pro"** (bigger / wider
   rings than the classic Akerfeldt one) or **"Sky Ring"** for the huge
   vertical rings. Download the `.udk` file.
3. Put the `.udk` file into:
   `…/rocketleague/TAGame/CookedPCConsole/` (Epic)
   or the equivalent Steam install path. BakkesMod's "Map Loader" tab in the
   `F2` menu will also show you the exact folder and let you load the map from
   the UI.
4. Start a **Freeplay** match (or Exhibition vs. nobody), then in BakkesMod →
   **Map** → pick the Lethamyr map from the list.

### The "small rings" map you already have

That's almost certainly the classic **Steam Workshop "Rings Map"** by
Akerfeldt (search `Rings Map` on the Steam Workshop). Keep it — it's the
best warm-up map for left/right air-roll rhythm. The Lethamyr Pro/Sky versions
are *next step*, not replacements.

## 2. Training-pack codes (in-game, Training → Custom → Browse Codes)

These are codes I'd trust at 1000 MMR. I'm **not listing codes I'm not 100%
sure of** — it's faster to search the pack name in-game than to chase a wrong
code. If a code below doesn't resolve, search the creator's YouTube channel
description and the code will be there.

| Purpose | Pack name (search by name on YouTube to get the current code) |
|---|---|
| Warm-up shots | **Poquito Rookie Training** |
| Redirects / first touch | **Wayton Pigeon — Striker: Redirects** |
| Air-dribble setups | **Wayton Pigeon — Aerial Shots** |
| Musty flicks | **Musty's Pack** (any of the shot-training ones) |
| Flip-reset reads | **Air-Dribble Launchers** (JHZER / Kevpert) |
| Ceiling shots | **Kevpert Ceiling Shots** |

**How to actually get the code:** open YouTube, search `"<pack name> training
pack code"`, grab the code from the pinned comment or description, paste into
Training → Custom → Browse → **Codes** tab.

## 3. Four-week progression

Do ~30 min of training before ranked. Don't grind a mechanic you can't even
**see the read for yet** — that's how you build bad muscle memory.

### Week 1 — car control floor

Goal: you stop coin-flipping kickoffs and 50s.

- **Freeplay, 10 min/day:** left-only air roll for 5 min, right-only for 5 min.
  No directional air roll yet. Just feel the car rotate both ways.
- **Small rings map (the one you have):** 2 runs a day. Don't try to be fast —
  hit every ring clean.
- **Kickoffs:** pick one kickoff style (speedflip or diagonal flip) and do
  20 reps in freeplay against the kickoff ghost. One style only. Switching
  styles every game is why most 1000s lose kickoff.
- **Ranked:** 3s. Rotate. If teammate is challenging, you go back post. That
  single habit is worth ~100 MMR at this level.

### Week 2 — air roll with purpose

Goal: you can hit a bouncing ball in the air without flipping by accident.

- **Lethamyr Ring Map: Pro** (the bigger rings) — 15 min. Use directional
  air roll (default bind is left-stick-based; rebind to a button like L1/LB
  if you haven't — most pros do).
- **Redirects pack** — 10 shots a day. The point isn't to score; it's to read
  where the ball is going *before* your first touch.
- **Freeplay:** 10 reps of "hit the ball, air roll 180°, recover facing ball."
  This is the foundation for ceiling reads.
- **Ranked:** 3s. Rule this week: **no double commits.** If you're second man
  and teammate is going, you stay.

### Week 3 — ceiling reads + first air dribbles

Goal: you can take a ball off the wall to the ceiling, and sometimes off.

- **Ceiling Shots pack** — 20 shots. You're not expected to score. You're
  training the *drive up the wall at the right speed* part.
- **Air-dribble launchers** — 10 reps from the launcher. Focus on the *second*
  touch, not the first.
- **Lethamyr Sky Ring** (or keep going with Ring Map Pro) — 10 min air roll.
- **Ranked:** 3s. Don't try air dribbles in ranked yet. Mechanics land in
  ranked ~3 weeks after they land in freeplay.

### Week 4 — flip reset entry

Goal: you can get a reset off a wall-read ~30% of the time in freeplay.

A flip reset requires **all four wheels** to touch the ball mid-air. At 1000
MMR the goal isn't to *score* flip resets — it's to train the ceiling read so
your ceiling shots and air dribbles get cleaner as a side effect.

- **Flip-reset pack (Air-Dribble Launchers)** — 15 min. Only goal: feel the
  "click" of the reset. Don't worry about the shot.
- **Freeplay:** ball on ceiling → drive up → get under it → reset. 20 reps.
- **Ring map:** 5 min warm-up each session, forever. This never stops being
  useful.
- **Ranked:** 3s. You can now *attempt* ceiling shots in ranked when the setup
  is free (opponent on the ground, ball rolling up your backboard).

## 4. Habits that move MMR more than mechanics

At 1000 MMR, 80% of losses are not mechanical. In priority order:

1. **Rotate back post, not ball-side.** If teammate is challenging, your job is
   the net, not the ball.
2. **Don't double-commit.** Count the committers out loud in your head.
3. **Slow down on offense.** The ball waits. Your teammates don't have time to
   rotate if you rush every 50/50.
4. **Shadow defense.** When last-back, drive *backwards* between the ball and
   your net. Don't dive.
5. **One post-game review per session.** Watch the last loss at 2x. Find one
   mistake you made twice. Next game, don't make it.

---

## 5. The minimum viable routine (if you only do one thing)

**10 min ring map (small) → 10 min ring map (Lethamyr Pro) → 10 shots of a
redirect pack → 3s ranked.**

That's it. Do that 4 days a week for a month and you will not be 1000 MMR
anymore.

---

## Appendix — why "codes" vs "maps" confuses everyone

- **Training-pack codes** (`XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX`): Psyonix's built-in system.
  Entered in Training → Custom → Browse → Codes. These are for *shots*, not
  *maps*.
- **Steam Workshop maps**: different system. You subscribe on Steam, they auto
  install, BakkesMod Workshop Loader lets you launch them from freeplay.
- **Lethamyr custom maps**: third system. Downloaded as `.udk`, dropped into
  the Rocket League install folder, loaded via BakkesMod. No code.

Lethamyr's "big rings" map not being findable by code isn't you missing
something — it genuinely doesn't have one.
